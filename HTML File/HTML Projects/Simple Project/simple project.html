Ronaldo’s first stint at Manchester United heralded 12 years of unscripted success at Madrid and Turin. On the back of winning 5 Balon D'ors, Drury was right to refer to him as being “beyond valuation, beyond forgery or imitation.” The time he spent in Spain and in Italy were years of countless records broken, and numerous awards collected. Bearing this in mind, it makes sense that Drury would view Ronaldo as being beyond forgery, being priceless and being beyond imitation. There is a cute and nostalgic allusion to the past; something akin to reliving numberless flashbacks of a young Ronaldo who mesmerised the Old Trafford faithful, and terrorised defenders. Upon this realisation, the goosebumps would be awash in renewed vigour. And it makes for a fitting precursor to the last line of Drury’s famous introduction; an epilogue that would jolt any nostalgic fan back to the present reality: “Now in his immaculate maturity. CR7 reunited”.
